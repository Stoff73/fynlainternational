Concept Explainer

What is an ISA?

The Short Answer

An Individual Savings Account (ISA) is a tax-free wrapper for your savings or investments. You can save up to £20,000 per tax year and pay no tax on the interest or investment returns.

Types of ISA

Cash ISA

Like a savings account, but interest is tax-free. Best for short-term goals or your emergency fund.

Stocks & Shares ISA

Invest in funds, shares, and bonds with no Capital Gains Tax or dividend tax. Best for 5+ year goals.

Lifetime ISA

Save up to £4,000/year towards your first home or retirement and get a 25% government bonus.

Innovative Finance ISA

Peer-to-peer lending within an ISA wrapper. Higher risk, potentially higher returns.

ISA Allowance 2025/26

£20,000 total across all ISAs per tax year.
You can split the allowance between different ISA types.
Lifetime ISA has a £4,000 sub-limit within the £20,000.

ISA Tips

Use it or lose it. Your annual allowance does not roll over to the next tax year.
Start early in the tax year. The sooner your money is sheltered, the more tax-free growth you capture.
Consider Stocks and Shares for longer-term goals (5+ years) where you can ride out short-term volatility.

Concept Explainer

What is Drawdown?

The Short Answer

Pension drawdown (also called flexi-access drawdown) lets you take income from your pension pot while keeping the rest invested. Unlike an annuity, your money stays invested and you choose how much to withdraw and when.

How It Works

You can take 25% of your pension pot tax-free (the tax-free lump sum).
The remaining 75% stays invested and you draw income as needed.
Income withdrawals are taxed as earnings at your marginal rate.
Your pot can grow or shrink depending on investment performance.
