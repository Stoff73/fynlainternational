## Problem Section

**Section headline**: "67" Is Not the Answer

- **State pension age isn't YOUR retirement age**: The government says 67 (rising to 68). But your retirement date depends on your money, not a political decision.
- **You've never done the real maths**: Adding up pension pots isn't enough. You need to factor in drawdown rates, inflation, state pension timing, investment growth, and how long your money needs to last.
- **Fear of getting it wrong**: Retire too early and you run out of money. Retire too late and you waste years you can't get back. The stakes are enormous, and most people are flying blind.

### Feature Cards

**1. Retirement Date Calculator**
Enter your pensions, savings, investments, and expected spending. Fynla calculates the earliest date you could retire while maintaining your lifestyle — and shows what changes if you adjust the variables.

**2. Income Bridge Modelling**
If you want to retire before state pension age, you need a bridge. Fynla models exactly how much you need from private sources to cover the gap until your state pension kicks in.

**3. Spending Scenarios**
Retirement spending isn't static. Model the "go-go, slow-go, no-go" years — higher spending in early retirement (travel, hobbies), reducing as you age, with potential care cost increases later.

**4. "What If" Levers**
Pull the levers that matter: What if I increase pension contributions by 2%? What if I downsize? What if I work part-time until 60? What if markets return 4% instead of 6%? See the impact on your retirement date in real time.

**5. Monte Carlo Confidence Score**
Don't just see one number. Fynla runs thousands of market scenarios and tells you the probability of your plan succeeding. "You have a 87% chance of not running out of money before age 95."

**6. Annual Check-In Tracker**
Your retirement date isn't set once — it evolves. Fynla tracks how your position changes year to year so you can see whether you're on track, ahead, or falling behind.

## FAQ Section

**Q: How accurate is the retirement date?**
A: The calculation is only as good as the data you provide and the assumptions used. Fynla uses evidence-based default assumptions for growth, inflation, and longevity — but lets you adjust all of them. The Monte Carlo analysis gives you a probability range rather than a single false-precision number.

**Q: Does this account for inflation?**
A: Yes. All projections are shown in today's money (real terms) by default, so you can understand what your retirement income actually buys. You can switch to nominal terms if you prefer.

**Q: What about early retirement / FIRE?**
A: Fynla is particularly useful for early retirement planning because it models the income bridge between stopping work and accessing state pension. It handles the complexity that simple FIRE calculators miss — like changing spending patterns, sequence of returns risk, and tax on different income sources.

**Q: Does it include state pension?**
A: Yes. Enter your state pension forecast (from gov.uk/check-state-pension) and Fynla models when it starts, how much you'll get, and what that means for your other drawdown requirements.

**Q: Can I model part-time work / semi-retirement?**
A: Yes. You can model reduced income over a transition period — for example, earning £20,000/year from part-time work between ages 58-63, then fully retiring.
