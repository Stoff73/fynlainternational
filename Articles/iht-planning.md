## Problem Section

**Section headline**: Most People Don't Know What They'll Owe

- **The £325,000 trap**: The nil rate band hasn't changed since 2009. Property prices have. If your estate is worth more than £325,000 (£500,000 with RNRB), your family pays 40% on the excess.
- **Pensions are changing**: From April 2027, unused pension pots will be included in your estate for IHT purposes. This is a massive shift that most people haven't planned for.
- **Guesswork isn't a strategy**: Gifting rules, taper relief, trust structures, business property relief — these are powerful tools, but only if you can see the numbers. Most people can't.

## FAQ Section

**Q: Is Fynla giving me tax advice?**
A: No. Fynla is a planning tool that helps you understand your IHT position based on the data you provide and current UK tax rules. For complex estate planning involving trusts or business structures, we'd recommend consulting a qualified adviser — and Fynla gives you the numbers to make that conversation much more productive.

**Q: How does Fynla handle the April 2027 pension changes?**
A: We've built the new pension inclusion rules into our modelling engine. You can toggle between current rules and post-April 2027 rules to see exactly how the change affects your estate.

**Q: Does this work for married couples / civil partners?**
A: Yes. Fynla models transferable nil rate bands, RNRB, and joint estate planning. You can see your position individually and as a couple.

**Q: How accurate is the IHT calculation?**
A: Fynla applies current HMRC rules including nil rate bands, RNRB, taper thresholds, and standard exemptions. The accuracy depends on the data you provide — the more complete your asset picture, the more accurate the result.

**Q: What about trusts and business property relief?**
A: Fynla covers the most common IHT planning strategies including gifting, pension drawdown, and standard exemptions. For complex trust structures or BPR claims, we'd recommend using Fynla's numbers alongside professional advice.
